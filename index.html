Clinical Evidence Management System

Module 1 — Evidence Triage

Before evidence can be documented, a writer must first decide when evidence is needed. In healthcare communication, not every statement functions the same way. Some claims are broad background knowledge, while others make specific assertions, rely on vague attribution, or offer guidance that should be traceable to evidence.

In this module, you will review incoming statements and determine whether they require documentation.

What You Are Learning to Notice
🌐

Broad Background Knowledge

Some statements express knowledge that is already widely established and may not require citation in every context.

  • general background information
  • broadly accepted ideas
  • statements not tied to a specific study or result
Reminder: Not every sentence needs citation. Good writers make judgments about what readers would reasonably expect to be documented.
📊

Specific Measurable Claims

Some statements report outcomes, percentages, comparisons, or findings that readers should be able to verify.

  • numbers or percentages
  • reported results
  • measurable effects
  • specific outcomes
Reminder: The more specific and testable a claim is, the more likely it needs traceable evidence.
🗣️

Vague Attribution

Some statements sound supported, but the source of support is unclear.

  • “experts say”
  • “research shows”
  • “studies suggest”
  • unclear authority references
Reminder: Evidence is not truly usable if the reader cannot tell where it came from.
🩺

Healthcare Guidance or Recommendation

Some statements advise action, treatment, prevention, or patient behavior. These usually need evidence because they shape real decisions.

  • recommendations
  • treatment advice
  • prevention strategies
  • care or behavior guidance
Reminder: In healthcare writing, recommendations should be linked to evidence that readers can check.
Key Principle

Evidence triage is the first step in responsible documentation. Before asking how to cite a source, a writer must first ask whether the claim actually requires evidence.

This is not about citing everything. It is about recognizing when readers need support that is credible, traceable, and professionally responsible.

When you are ready, begin the triage scan and evaluate each statement as it enters the system.

Model Documentation Statement

Clinical claims should include traceable evidence so readers can verify whether the claim is supported, judge the credibility of the information, and locate the original source. In healthcare communication, this matters because recommendations and factual assertions can influence real decisions, so evidence must be professionally responsible as well as accurate.
